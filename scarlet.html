Scarlet Flax

  • Annual flowering herbs has five scarlet petals and stamens that are covered in blue pollen.
  • Each flower lasts only for a few hours, but continues to bloom throughout the day. Scarlet flax wildflowers grow from 1 to 2 feet and last about four to six weeks,between the months of April and September.
  • Native to North Africa and southern Europe
  • USDA plant haardiness zones 3 through 10
  • They love ull sun and can tolerate extreme heat, but prefer cooler climates
  • Scarlet Flax seeds produce high levels of linseed oil
    • It's used in baking and in bulk forming laxative
    • Linoleum(inexpensive floor covering) is produced from linseed oil.
    • Flax fiber(stronge than cotton) is taken from the skin of the stem and is used for linen fabric, rope and twine
  • Care is minimal and flower is fairly easy to growa nd maintain
